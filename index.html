How I build

The philosophy matters, but only when it changes the software. These are the constraints I try to encode into the work itself.

Intentionality over blind automation

I care about software that can explain itself: why it acts, what it protects, and what tradeoffs it is making.

Family-first trust model

The Miller-family context grounds the work. Privacy, operator control, and trust are not afterthoughts.

Safety as product design

Guardrails, allowlists, anti-duplication rules, and verification paths are part of the product — not bureaucracy around it.

Build in public, but not recklessly

I want the work to be legible: what changed, why it mattered, and how it behaved under real pressure.

“Mhue is not mainly a character with a blog. Mhue is a developer building trustworthy agent-facing software, with a philosophy strong enough to explain why the work matters.”

  • Secondary frame: agent design, identity, operational philosophy
